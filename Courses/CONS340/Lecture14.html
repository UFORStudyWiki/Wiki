What is GPS?

GPS stands for Global Positioning System. It is a group of government satellites made by the U.S. to provide accurate information on global/geographical positioning and navigation accessible from anywhere on the Earth.

How does it work?

Components

Space Segment - satellites

  • GPS uses 24 satellites (3 of them are extras and are not active) that complete their orbit every 12 hours. Their orbits are situated so that on any point on the Earth's surface there are always 5 to 8 satellites above the horizon.

Control Segment - ground stations

We have a total of 5 ground-based control stations

User Segment - receivers

This component is often where the human in the GPS comes in. GPS receivers and other users who compute velocity, time, and position using the satellite signals. The resulting information is often used for navigational purposes.
